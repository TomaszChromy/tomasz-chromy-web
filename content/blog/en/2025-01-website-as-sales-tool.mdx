Most companies treat their website like a business card. "We need a website because everyone has one." Result? A website that does nothing. Doesn't generate inquiries, doesn't build trust, doesn't support sales.

Successful companies think differently. In this article, I'll show you how to change perspective and turn your website into a real business tool.

## Two Approaches to Websites

### Approach 1: Website as Business Card

- "We need to have a website"
- Goal: be present on the internet
- Success metric: "looks nice"
- After launch: forget about it

**Result:** Website exists, but does nothing.

### Approach 2: Website as Sales Tool

- "Website should attract customers"
- Goal: generate inquiries and support sales
- Success metric: number of leads, conversion
- After launch: optimization and development

**Result:** Website works for business 24/7.

## How Do Successful Companies Think?

### 1. Website Has a Specific Goal

Not "be on the internet," but:
- Generate quote requests
- Sign up for newsletter
- Book demos
- Sell products

One main goal = clear strategy.

### 2. Website Is Part of Sales Process

An effective website doesn't work in isolation:

**Awareness** → Ads, SEO, social media  
**Interest** → Homepage, landing page  
**Consideration** → Offer, portfolio, testimonials  
**Decision** → Form, contact, CTA  
**Loyalty** → Newsletter, blog, remarketing

### 3. Website Answers Customer Questions

Before contacting you, customers want to know:
- What exactly do you do?
- Who do you help?
- How much does it cost (roughly)?
- Why should I trust you?
- What does collaboration look like?

An effective website answers these questions before they're asked.

### 4. Website Builds Trust

People don't buy from strangers. Website must build credibility:
- Client testimonials
- Portfolio with real projects
- Case studies with results
- Owner/team faces
- Proof of competence

### 5. Website Is Measured and Optimized

Successful companies track:
- Where traffic comes from
- Which pages are most visited
- Where users leave
- How many forms are filled
- What's the cost per lead

And make changes based on this data.

## Elements of a Sales Website

### Hero That Convinces

- Clear message: what you do and for whom
- Benefit: what customer gains
- CTA: what to do now

### Benefits Section

Not features ("responsive website"), but benefits ("Your customers buy easily from their phones").

### Social Proof

- Testimonials with name and company
- Client logos
- Specific numbers and results
- Case studies

### Clear CTA

- In contrasting color
- With specific text
- Repeated several times

### Simple Form

- Minimum fields
- Clear what happens after sending
- Confirmation of submission

## Typical "Business Card" Mistakes

### Talking About Yourself Instead of Customer

"We are industry leaders," "We have 20 years of experience"

Customer doesn't care. They care about: **what they'll gain**.

### No CTA

User doesn't know what to do. Doesn't see a button. Leaves.

### Hidden Information

Prices, process, FAQ – everything needs searching. No one searches. Leaves.

### Stock Photos

Smiling people in suits. Build nothing.

### No Updates

Copyright 2019, last post 3 years ago. "Are they still operating?"

## How to Turn Business Card Into Sales Tool?

### Step 1: Define Goal

What should the website do? One main goal.

### Step 2: Know Your Customer

- Who are they?
- What problems do they have?
- What are they looking for?
- What will convince them?

### Step 3: Rewrite Content

- From "we" to "you"
- From features to benefits
- From generalities to specifics

### Step 4: Add Proof

- Client testimonials
- Case studies
- Portfolio
- Numbers

### Step 5: Clear CTA

- In hero
- After each section
- At page end

### Step 6: Measure and Improve

- Google Analytics
- Heatmaps
- A/B tests
- Form analysis

## Summary

A website can be:
- **Business card** – exists, but does nothing
- **Sales tool** – generates inquiries 24/7

The difference? Mindset and a few key elements:
- Clear goal
- Benefit-focused message
- Trust proof
- Clear CTA
- Measurement and optimization

Which version works for your business?
